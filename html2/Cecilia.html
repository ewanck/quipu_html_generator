My name is Cesilia Pena Guerrero from Ingano Grande village, in Sondorillo. I was sterilized when my daughter was one month old. I went to the medical centre to register her, and they wouldn't give me the paperwork I needed to register her in Sondorillo. They didn't give it to me, they said that I should be sterilised, or that my husband should be sterilised. But he didn't want to. They said it was harder to get men to have the surgery, that it was better if they sterilized me instead. I didn' want to. I was afraid, afraid of the operation. And so they encouraged me. They said they would help us out.And then they brought us to Huancabamba, and I had the surgery there.But they never remembered the promises they made, we have received nothing, nothing. And that's why we are here now, us and other other ladies here, we want some help so that we can support ourselves. My children are at school and I can hardly work. My belly hurts, my brain hurts, my head hurts. I don't live well.
