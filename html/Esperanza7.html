Well, my name is Esperanza Huayama Aguirre, from the village of Rodeo Pampa, very far away from here.Well I …when a committee  from Lima arrived, many friends and I …they took several of us to the policlinic,they gathered more than a hundred women there that day when that committee came, it was like being in a prison.They closed the gate, and when some women saw how others were crying with the painthey wanted to leave, but we were already inside and they wouldn't let us leave.  They took me inside and drew blood from my finger for tests and then they brought me to do the sterilisation and gave me the anaesthetic. I was just falling asleep as they started cutting me and it hurt …I heard them saying 'she's is pregnant'Then I, because God didn't want my baby to die at that moment, I said to them: 'I don't want you to remove my baby, I'd rather die with my baby, leave my baby there.'They put the anesthesia again and after that I don't remember anything. I remember around 6 pm with a tremendous pain,that they had made us lie down head to feet, 2 in a single bed.And I woke up with tremendous pain, the IV line had come out of my arm. Me with tremendous pain, and they threw us out and told us we all had to go home.And a lady that had also been cut badly was sent to Piura and she had died in Piura. And we ended up …I was 3 months pregnant when they did it, and until I was 7 months there was a doctor that checked on me, his name was Guerrero. That doctor, very angrily said that those doctors had come and didn't make us healthy but had made a damage by cutting our bellies. Some women had ended up really bad, and he kept checking on me every 3 days, every 3 days. I spent a lot, thank God my husband he got the money so that I could be a bit better. My belly had swollen and I used to sleep like sitting downI suffered with my little child like this until 7 months.I wasn't at all well, the belly was very swollen and the doctor was checking on me,and he told me it was better if I went to Piura because here they wouldn't be able toI went to say goodbye to my children, I had 7 small children. I said goodbye to them to go to PiuraThey took me to my village, Rodeo Pampaaround 1 in the morning I woke up with a tremendous pain andI stood up and huge gush of blood came out of me, like a haemorrhage.And then with a midwife, because in the countryside there are midwifes and they also fix our bellies, until they brought me to Huancabamba. And …around 6, 7 in the morning, I don't remember much, but the midwife had already helped me give birth. They had seen him and pulled him out with the doctor. Well, my child he died,and I was really unwell and I'm still really unwell. It gets swollen when I make effort, my belly gets swollen,my waist hurts. I can't go to the country anymore cause I can't strain myself. I can still do soft thingsbut I can't be in the country, it hurts me. This is why us peasant women, because we were illiteratethey did what they wanted to us.But now we say that these things that have damaged all the women in this areamust not happen again. In Huarmaca everyone is bad, we are all bad. The women feel awful.And we have this experience and we won't allow other young women to go through it,we won't allow this to happen again. That is why we are asking for women's rights. Thank you.
